NYC terror attack: Lin-Manuel Miranda, Mariah Carey, more celebs send love to the city

Anika Reed

USA TODAY

Celebrities are sending their love and support to New York on social media following a terror attack that left eight people dead Tuesday when a truck plowed down pedestrians on a bicycle path near the World Trade Center in Lower Manhattan.

Josh Groban said he and he his dog were just half a block from the scene of the attack. "Oh my god I just heard gun shots and ran with my dog. Downtown. (expletive)," he wrote in a string of tweets.

Sam Smith took to Twitter to give his condolences, writing, "New York. My heart is with you always and forever. Devastating news."

New York natives Lin-Manuel Miranda, Mariah Carey and Barbra Streisand sent their love to the city, with Miranda writing, "NY, I love you so much. You are strong and full of everyday heroes and I love you."

Lena Dunham asked her "five borough friends" to stay safe after the attack.

Bette Midler sent her "deepest condolences" to the families of the victims.

Stranger Things star David Harbour called the incident "unsettling."

More messages of love came from John Legend and Jane the Virgin star Gina Rodriguez.

Billy Eichner recognized his high school in photos from the scene, tweeting out about the "horrifying" event and sending his love.

Josh Gad and Zoe Saldana sent their condolences to the people affected in Tuesday's attack, with Gad tweeting that he "can't stomach this non-stop senseless hatred and violence."
